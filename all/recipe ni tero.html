Sinigang na Bangus

Serves 4–5 • Prep 15 min • Cook 20 min
Filipino
Sour (Tamarind)
Fish
Jump to: Ingredients • Steps • Notes

Ingredients

  • 1 whole bangus (milkfish), cleaned and sliced
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 medium labanos (daikon), sliced
  • 10–12 pcs sitaw (string beans), cut 2"
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 1–2 pcs siling haba (optional)
  • 1 pack sinigang mix or 1 cup fresh sampalok broth
  • 2 tbsp patis (fish sauce) or to taste
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • 5 cups water

Steps

  1. In a pot, bring 5 cups of water to a boil with onion and tomatoes. Simmer 5 minutes.
  2. Add labanos, sitaw, and eggplant. Cover and simmer 5–7 minutes until half-cooked.
  3. Gently add bangus slices (to avoid breaking). Simmer 5–8 minutes until cooked through.
  4. Stir in sinigang mix or fresh tamarind broth and fish sauce. Add siling haba if using.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning. Add kangkong, simmer 1 minute, then turn off heat.

Notes

Use fresh sampalok for a cleaner, more natural sourness. If you prefer less fish oil in the broth, remove some of the fish skin before simmering. Swap bangus for tilapia or shrimp if desired.

Nutritional tip

Sinigang is low in calories and high in vegetables — keep the salt moderate and use more vegetables for bulk.

Variations
  • Use fresh sampalok instead of mix
  • Add gabi (taro) for thicker broth
  • Make it spicy with siling labuyo
